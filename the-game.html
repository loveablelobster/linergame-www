The Liner Shipping GameTM versions, main features and learning objectives

Based on the 3 different ground versions of the setup, game sessions are designed to match aspects of interest of specific target audiences. Typical target audiences for game sessions will be e.g:

  • Students in shipping and logistics

  • New employees in shipping / logistics companies

  • Support functions in shipping / logistics

  • Experienced shipping / logistics professionals

  • Freight Forwarders, 3PL providers and shippers

  • Ports and canals

  • Equipment and software vendors

  • Financial, insurance, classification companies

  • Shipping / Logistics Executives

  • Professionals from other industries

A game session is designed to run as an isolated activity for ½-1 day with an educational purpose, but will often also be part of training and course programs of longer duration. The game can potentially be used for a range of other purposes, e.g. as a business process simulation or change management tool in company business transformation projects or as a recruiting tool to test skills and behavior of candidates potentially to be hired in a company

History of The Liner Shipping Game TM

The Liner Shipping GameTM was developed in a first version during 2012-2014 by Aalborg University, Ange Optimization and Maersk Line as a part of a research project on revenue management sponsored by the Danish Maritime Fund.

  • Over the years it has been continuously improved in order maximize the learning intensity of the game play, improve realism and keep it up-to-date with the state-of-the-art management principles of the modeled processes and industry challenges. To know more about the history and ongoing research on the game and it’s use in practice, click here (http://www.shipping-logistics.aau.dk) for further information
